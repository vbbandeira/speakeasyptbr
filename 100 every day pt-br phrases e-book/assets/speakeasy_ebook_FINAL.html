How to use
this phrasebook

04

This is not meant to be read like a textbook. Use it like a tool — pick a small set of phrases, practice them out loud, and reuse them in real life.

⏱ The 10-minute routine
1
Choose 10 phrases from one chapter.
2
Read them out loud twice — slow, then natural speed.
🎧
Audio Kit: press play → listen → repeat out loud
3
Record yourself on your phone and compare.
🎧
Audio Kit: compare your recording with the natural-speed track
4
Swap one word and repeat — e.g., 'Eu quero água' → 'Eu quero café'.
5
Use at least 1 phrase today in a message or conversation.
💡
Quick tip
If you feel understood but still don't sound natural, it's usually pronunciation + rhythm, not vocabulary. The Audio Kit was made exactly for that.

Chapter I

Greetings &
First Impressions

Saudações e primeiras impressões

05

Brazilians are warm and expressive — a good greeting goes a long way. Learn these and you'll feel at home from day one.

#🇧🇷 PT-BR🇺🇸 English
1
Oi!
Hi!Very casual — used among friends
2
Olá!
Hello!Neutral, works in any context
3
Bom dia!
Good morning!
4
Boa tarde!
Good afternoon!
5
Boa noite!
Good evening / nightUsed as both greeting and goodbye at night
6
Tudo bem?
How are you?Lit. "All good?" — very common
7
Tudo certo?
Everything ok?
8
E você?
And you?
9
Prazer em te conhecer!
Nice to meet you!
10
Com licença.
Excuse me.To get attention or pass by someone
💡
Quick tip
Brazilians often reply to "Tudo bem?" with "Tudo bem!" — it's both the question and the answer. Think of it as "How's it going?" / "Good, good!"

Saying "Mais devagar, por favor" is not a sign of weakness — it shows you're serious about learning. Native speakers will respect it every time.
