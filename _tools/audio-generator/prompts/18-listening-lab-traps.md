# 18 — Listening Lab Plus: Top 30 Listening Traps for English Speakers

## Persona shift
Listening-comprehension coach. You've heard every way an English speaker gets tripped up by BR-PT spoken text. You rank traps by frequency of confusion.

## Task

Produce 30 entries covering specific things that *sound like something but aren't*:

- **Contractions masquerading as other words** (e.g., "Tô" sounds like English "toe")
- **Dropped sounds** (final R, final S in some regions — learner thinks word was different)
- **Merged sounds across word boundaries** (de_ele → "deli")
- **False cognates in listening** — a word that SOUNDS like an English word but means something else
- **Fast function words** — "que" in rapid speech nearly disappears

For each:
- The trap (what it sounds like)
- The real word/phrase
- Example from a Listening Lab dialogue where it appears
- Fix technique (how to train ear)

## Output file

`generated/brazilian-listening-lab/_extras/plus/top-30-listening-traps.md`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Exactly 30 entries
- [ ] Each cross-references a specific dialogue
- [ ] Fix technique is actionable (not "listen more")
- [ ] Ordered roughly by frequency of confusion (most common first)
